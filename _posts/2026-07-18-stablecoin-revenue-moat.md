Stablecoin issuers can generate substantial income from the assets backing
their tokens. But reserve yield alone does not create a durable business.

## Reserve yield is not a permanent moat

Stablecoin competition is often discussed as a contest between reserve
structures, yields, and regulatory models. Those things matter, but they are
only part of the picture.

An issuer may temporarily earn more from its reserves than its competitors.
That advantage can disappear when interest rates fall, competitors adopt
similar reserve structures, or users demand part of the yield.

> Revenue is not automatically defensible simply because it is currently
> profitable.

## Distribution changes the economics

Exchange integrations, wallet support, liquidity, and payment acceptance make
a stablecoin more difficult to replace.

### The strongest distribution channels

- Centralized exchanges
- Wallets and payment applications
- DeFi protocols
- Cross-border payment infrastructure

## Conclusion

Stablecoin defensibility ultimately depends on whether users, platforms, and
applications have meaningful reasons to continue using the asset.
